Should the attorney-client privilege be abolished?
Attorney-client privilege upholds justice by improving legal access.
Without attorney-client privilege, people would not tell their lawyers anything.
The client's interests would be harmed without the client-attorney privilege.
The predictability in the application of the privilege protection is critical to its effectiveness and success.
An higher judicial willingness to remove the privilege may damage compliance.
Attorney-client privilege obstructs justice.
Attorney-client privilege does not cause clients to divulge all information.
Rules of law should not be designed to protect the guilty.
There are many situations in which attorney-client privilege can/should be restricted.
