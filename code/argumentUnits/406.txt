Should the UK have a Bill of Rights?
People have certain fundamental rights, the infringement of which should be illegal.
Changes to a Bill of Rights can be made by amendments.
A bill of rights would enshrine principles that will change in the future.
The lack of a Bill of Rights is not a real problem.
The concept of 'rights' is problematic when multiple rights are infringed.
An unchanging bill of rights can enshrine bad principles.
