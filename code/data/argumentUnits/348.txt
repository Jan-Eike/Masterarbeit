This House Believes that the World Bank is an obstacle to development
The World Bank investment policy consolidates the position of the corrupt, inefficient and antidemocratic regimes of many developing countries.
The contribution of donor countries to the World Bank is a poor investment.
The public infrastructure projects funded by the IBRD have consistently been recognized as unmitigated disasters.
The loan strategy of the IBRD is inherently risky.
The World Bank correctly prioritises economic development over other social values.
The World Bank should not seek its justification in being a financially sound investment for the donor countries.
The new objectives of the WB will improve the quality of lives of the populations of developing countries.
The strategy for financing the loans of the IBRD is remarkably efficient.
